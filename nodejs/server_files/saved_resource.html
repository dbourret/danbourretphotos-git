    Professional Photo Restoration

    Photo restoration can repair many common forms of age and damage, including fading, scratches, stains, tears, creases, discoloration, dust, and small missing areas.

    The goal is not to make an old photograph look artificial. The goal is to restore visible detail, improve clarity, and preserve the natural appearance and history of the original image.

    Damage Repair

    Repair scratches, tears, cracks, folds, creases, dust, stains, and other physical damage.

    Fading & Color Correction

    Improve faded colors, restore contrast, correct color casts, and recover details that have become difficult to see.

    Missing Area Reconstruction

    Rebuild small missing or damaged areas using surrounding image details while keeping the repair natural.

    Black-and-White Restoration

    Improve tone, contrast, sharpness, and visible detail in older black-and-white photographs.

    Portrait Cleanup

    Carefully improve faces, clothing, backgrounds, and other important details without an overly processed appearance.

    Print Preparation

    Prepare the completed restoration as a high-resolution digital file suitable for sharing, archiving, or professional printing.

    How the Process Works

    1. Send a clear scan or photograph of the original image.
    2. Describe the damage and any specific changes you would like.
    3. The photograph will be reviewed and you will receive a project quote.
    4. Restoration begins after the scope and price are approved.
    5. The completed restoration is delivered as a high-resolution digital file.

    What to Send

    For the best result, provide the highest-quality scan or photograph available.

    • Scan the original at 600 DPI when possible
    • Use TIFF, PNG, or a high-quality JPEG
    • Avoid screenshots or images downloaded from social media
    • Keep the original photograph flat and evenly lit
    • Do not apply filters or automatic enhancements before sending it

    Restoration Pricing

    Pricing depends on the level of damage, the complexity of the repair, the amount of missing detail, and the final size needed. You will receive a quote before any restoration work begins.
